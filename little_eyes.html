A camera is not the product.
Perspective is.

The missing perspective

Most childhood photos are taken by adults. They capture what parents thought mattered, not what the child actually noticed. A child’s true perspective is rarely preserved.

Our design philosophy

Less technology

No screens. No algorithms. No social sharing. The camera does less so memories can mean more.

Child autonomy

Children decide what matters. There is no instruction on what to capture.

Designed by limits

Constraints are not flaws. They are intentional design decisions.

An object, not a gadget

Small, durable, and personal. A memory object designed to live in a child’s hand, not on a screen.

Beyond the camera

One day, these images may become archives, books, or exhibitions — a cultural record of how children once saw the world.
